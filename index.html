BookCrush — Case Study

Overview

Finding your next
literary obsession

A full end-to-end UX project from identifying a real user problem to delivering an interactive prototype. BookCrush is a Tinder-style book recommendation app designed to cut through choice overload and connect readers with books that genuinely match their taste.

The Problem

Readers are saturated with options and paradoxically cannot decide what to read next. More recommendations means more paralysis.

The Solution

Based on genre, tropes and mood, the app surfaces a small curated set of daily matches via swipe-based interaction.

The User

Women readers aged 20–35, active on social reading platforms, who want recommendations beyond trending titles.

The Goal

Connect readers with their next favourite book. Reduce frustration by replacing infinite lists with focused discovery.

Problem Validation

The problem was already
everywhere

Before any formal research, social media confirmed the problem was real. Readers were not struggling with access to books — they were drowning in too many choices and not enough signal.

Research Conclusions

What the research
actually said

1
Readers want a tool that respects their independence while helping them decide — not one that pushes trending titles.
2
No existing app lets users reject books, filter by tropes, or preview tone before committing. Critical unmet need.
3
Social features are valued as an optional layer, not as the core. Primary experience must feel personal.
4
The ideal design is clean, intuitive, and visually appealing — focused on book content, not social networking.

MVP Definition

What to build first —
and what to earn later

Must-have for v1

Account login — everything personalised to the user
Book explorer: genre, tropes, pace, emotional tone, ending type
Book status: want to read / left it / read / favourite
Emotional reviews with tags like “felt like a hug”
Affinity algorithm based on reads and trope preferences
Custom tags (“for when I need to cry”, “read before sleeping”)

Nice-to-have for v2+

Mood picker — recs based on how you feel today
Shared reading mode with chapter discussion
First pages preview before deciding
Sensitive content / trigger warnings from community
Book recognition by photo
Kindle Unlimited / Scribd integration
